Welcome

Hello! I am a Computer Science student at the University of Colorado Boulder, uniquely positioned at the intersection of Software Engineering and Data Analytics. In an era where data is the new oil, I have chosen to build a dual foundation: the technical discipline to engineer robust systems and the analytical insight to extract tangible value from the information those systems generate.

My background isn't just about writing code—it’s about solving problems for people. Through my current work with Boulder Artemis Consulting, I act as a bridge between complex data infrastructures and actionable business insights. Whether I am optimizing a data lake in Microsoft Fabric or developing a test-driven Java application like "Deckfall," my goal remains the same: to create tools that are as functional as they are insightful. I believe that the best software doesn't just "work"—it empowers the user to make better decisions.

This digital portfolio serves as more than just a landing page; it is a 360-degree view of my capabilities. In a digital landscape where personal brands often feel like "hollow robots," I want to show the human behind the keyboard. Beyond the repositories on my GitHub and the milestones on my LinkedIn, you will find a narrative of my technical growth and my personal interests. Visitors will find a deep dive into my academic projects, my contributions to the Society of Women Engineers (SWE), and a glimpse into the hobbies that keep my creativity sharp, from pottery to cooking and gaming. I am currently seeking opportunities for Summer 2026 that will allow me to apply this hybrid skillset to real-world challenges.

Deckfall Project
Deckfall
"Deckfall" is a Java-based deck-building roguelike inspired by titles like Slay the Spire. This project allows me to apply Object-Oriented Design (OOD) and Test-Driven Development (TDD). Using Java2D and Swing, my partner and I are navigating the full software design cycle, focusing on clean code, inheritance, and modularity to create a scalable gaming experience.
Java TDD
